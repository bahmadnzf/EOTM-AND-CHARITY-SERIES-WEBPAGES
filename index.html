WHAT WE DO

The Next Generation of Change Agents

Founded in 2013, Executives Helping Initiative is a youth-led movement of 700+ volunteers fighting poverty through action.

We don't just raise awareness. We implement solutions.

we show up at orphanages, prisons, hospitals, and underserved communities across Lagos—delivering food, medical care, hope, and second chances.

OUR IMPACT

In August 2018, EHI was recognized by SME 100 Nigeria for its invaluable contributions to active citizenry and engagement in Nigeria.

  • → 30+ orphanages visited monthly since 2013
  • → 20+ communities transformed with food, clothing & medical aid
  • → 700+ youth volunteers empowered as leaders
  • → Prison reform initiatives bringing dignity to inmates
  • → Mental health advocacy supporting those battling depression

What makes us different?
We're a leadership incubator disguised as a charity. Every volunteer leads projects, builds skills, and becomes a change agent.

Dare Asobele

Founder & Visionary

Lawyer Project Manager Change Catalyst

Timothy-Asobele Emmanuel Dare, known simply as Dare Asobele. Dare founded EHI in 2013 as a law student at the University of Lagos after visiting an orphanage and realizing good intentions weren't enough. Inspired by legal giants Chief Gani Fawehinmi and Judge Chukwudifu Oputa, he rallied five friends and turned compassion into a national movement.

What sparked this journey? Anger turned into action.

Called to the Nigerian Bar (2015) | PMP® Certified

Also founded ASADS (Anti-Suicide and Depression Squad)

"The world just needs people willing to take action."
